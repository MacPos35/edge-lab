# Council review 2026-07-12 — performance & loss study, improvement plan

Second sitting of the council (first: design review 2026-07-05). Seats: **Model**,
**Data/Stats**, **Dashboard**, **Integration/Ops**, plus the **Contrarian** (per
`../polymarket-edge-lab/KILL_CRITERIA.md` tradition). Convened after the last
quarterfinal, with 10 graded knockout predictions and 100 played matches in the feed;
4 matches remain (two semifinals, bronze, final — SF1 France v Spain, SF2 England v
Argentina by FIFA numbering).

All numbers below come from `backtest.py`, a leakage-free harness that (a) exactly
reproduces every frozen prediction from the scoreboard (worst diff 0.0000 across all
10), then (b) re-scores the tournament one-step-ahead under alternative parameters.

## 1. Performance so far

### Loss — knockout P(advance), the pre-registered track record (n = 10)

| Variant | Mean binary Brier | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Blend (headline, K=6) | **0.1983** | 6/10 favorites advanced |
| Pure Elo (w=0) | **0.1553** | beats the headline |
| Pure tournament form (w=1) | **0.2899** | clearly worst |
| Coin flip reference | 0.2500 | |

The blend beats a coin flip but **loses to its own Elo-only sanity variant**. The gap
is not spread evenly: it is almost entirely two matches where tournament form was hot
and wrong — Brazil v Norway (blend 0.70 on Brazil, w1 said 0.90; Norway won; +0.191
Brier vs w0) and Mexico v England (blend 0.55 on host Mexico, w1 said 0.80; England
won; +0.165). In 6 of 10 matches the blend actually edged w0 slightly. Diagnosis:
attack/defense multipliers fit on n≈4–5 games chase variance, exactly the failure mode
the design capped with shrinkage — the cap just wasn't tight enough.

Calibration of the 10 graded picks: mean P(favorite advances) 0.653 vs realized 0.600
— mild overconfidence, small sample.

### Loss — 90' W/D/L over the whole tournament (n = 92 one-step-ahead)

The daily grading only ever scored the ~14 knockout matches, but the model implicitly
predicted all 100. Backtesting W/D/L at 90' one-step-ahead (fit strictly on
earlier-finished matches, first 8 skipped as burn-in):

| Model | Log loss | Multiclass Brier |
|---|---|---|
| Deployed (K=6, scale 850) | 0.8933 | 0.5259 |
| Best swept (K=30, scale 600) | 0.8727 | ~0.522 |
| Pure Elo, scale 850 | 0.8961 | 0.5277 |
| Hindsight base rates (46/27/27) | 1.0661 | 0.6439 |
| Uniform ⅓/⅓/⅓ | 1.0986 | 0.6667 |

The model has real skill (~0.17 nats better than climatology). Both loss surfaces
point the same direction — **more shrinkage and a sharper Elo scale** — but a paired
t-test of per-match log loss (deployed vs K=15/scale 600) gives t ≈ 1.0, n = 92:
**directionally consistent, not statistically significant.**

Calibration findings on the 92: predicted draw rate 0.245 vs observed 0.280 (within
noise, se ≈ 0.045); favorites in the 0.6–0.8 bucket realized 0.81 vs predicted 0.67
(n=26) — the same "Elo gaps deserve more weight" signal as the scale sweep.

## 2. Parameter-by-parameter study

| Parameter | Deployed | Sweep verdict |
|---|---|---|
| `SHRINK_K` | 6 | WDL loss minimized at 9–15 (flat); KO Brier improves monotonically toward w=0. Deployed value gives tournament form too much say. **Main lever.** |
| `ELO_SCALE` | 850 | WDL log loss minimized at ~600 (0.8787 vs 0.8933); KO agrees (smaller is better everywhere in the joint sweep). 850 flattens real gaps. **Second lever.** |
| `RHO` (Dixon-Coles) | −0.10 | Flat: −0.20…0.0 moves log loss by <0.01. Hardcoding was the right call; keep. |
| `MU_TOTAL` | 2.7 | Observed FT-match mean is 2.92; sweep mildly prefers 2.9–3.1 (Δlogloss <0.001/step). Not worth touching mid-tournament. |
| `HOST_ELO_BONUS` | 50 | WDL wants more, KO wants 0 — contradictory, i.e. noise. Only 1–2 host matches remain (none: all hosts eliminated). Keep. |
| `ET_FRACTION`, `PENALTY_P` | ⅓, 0.50 | Only 3 of 10 graded matches reached ET — untestable at this n. Keep. |
| `LAMBDA_FLOOR`, `MAX_GOALS` | 0.25, 8 | Never binding at observed λ ∈ [0.65, 2.27]. Keep. |
| `N_SIMS` | 10,000 | MC se on a 35% champion prob ≈ 0.5 pt, below the model's ±10 pt honesty bar. Keep. |
| Frozen end-2025 Elo snapshot | — | Design choice to avoid double-counting; the w0-beats-blend result *validates* it. Keep frozen. |

Joint sweep (log loss / KO Brier) confirms no bad interaction: the K=15–30 ×
scale 500–600 corner is best on both metrics simultaneously.

## 3. Council deliberation

- **Model:** The evidence is coherent across two independent loss surfaces: shrink
  harder (K≈15), sharpen the scale (≈600). Wants the tuned parameters adopted.
- **Contrarian:** A sweep over collected data is in-sample hyperparameter selection
  even when each prediction is one-step-ahead. t ≈ 1.0. n(KO) = 10. Changing the
  headline mid-experiment on that evidence is the "overfitting theater" the code
  comments warned about, and it would fork the 14-match track record into two
  regimes. The design already built the correct instrument for this: graded
  variants. Use it.
- **Data/Stats:** Sides with the Contrarian on the headline, with Model on the
  instrument: add the tuned model as a third *tracked* variant so the remaining 4
  matches grade it truly out-of-sample. Also: the pipeline discards ~90% of its
  statistical power by grading only knockout P(advance) — the 90' W/D/L backtest is
  now committed as `backtest.py` so the post-tournament writeup can use all 100
  matches.
- **Dashboard:** Surface the new variant in the existing "blend sanity" line only;
  no layout changes, no new headline numbers.
- **Integration/Ops:** Any change must land before the run that first sees SF events
  with confirmed teams (ESPN can publish them any time now — semifinals are ~Jul
  14–15), or the variant misses the semifinals. Zero risk to the hourly pipeline:
  additive JSON keys, selftest extended, dashboard only embeds the fragment.
- **All seats on polymarket-edge-lab:** frozen per `KILL_CRITERIA.md` — zero feature
  work until readout. This review deliberately looked only at collection health,
  **not** at the holdout edge: 556 usable resolved markets (min 200 already met),
  ~50–100 resolving/day, snapshots current through this morning. On this pace the
  2026-08-19 readout will be comfortably powered. No action, no peeking.

## 4. Plan

**Adopted now (deployed with this commit):**
1. Headline model **unchanged** — the frozen track record stays one regime through
   the final.
2. New tracked variant `p_adv_cv` (K=15, Elo scale 600) issued and graded alongside
   `w0`/`w1` for every remaining match; shown in the sanity line of the tab. This is
   the out-of-sample test of today's sweep.
3. `backtest.py` committed (reproduces all frozen predictions exactly, worst diff
   0.0000) so the sweep is re-runnable and auditable.

**Post-tournament (after Jul 19, in the writeup):**
4. Grade the council variant's 4-match record honestly, whatever it says.
5. Full-power evaluation: 90' W/D/L log loss over all ~104 matches, plus the final
   champion-odds log score of each daily forecast against the actual champion.
6. Record the K/scale conclusion as a prior for any future tournament model, where
   it would be a *pre-registered* choice rather than a mid-stream patch.

**Rejected:**
- Changing `SHRINK_K`/`ELO_SCALE` on the headline (Contrarian, carried 4–1).
- Bivariate Poisson / negative binomial upgrade: 4 matches left, no sample to
  validate against, pure complexity.
- Refitting `RHO`, `MU_TOTAL`, `HOST_ELO_BONUS`: flat or contradictory sweeps.
- Anything touching polymarket-edge-lab before the pre-registered readout.
